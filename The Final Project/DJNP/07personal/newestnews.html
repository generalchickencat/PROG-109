Eb's Newest Reviews News

6/8/3018

Ebauchon has the newest round-up of Reviews for last year's collaboration albums up. Check out some of the blurbs below:


Crustbox

"Crustbox doesn't open itself easily. This album is like homework (though not like Daft Punk's Homework). It's a study in noise, and requires patience. This is something that's going to click all of a sudden when you've thrown it on in the background on your millionth commute to work - you'll suddenly emerge on the other side of the hole in a bright place with a beautiful geometry only the few can see. Consider this level 11 stuff. One day, this album will be absolutely necessary."
-- We Are Gazillions Issue 12 Vol. 2

Witchita Slayers

"This smash-bang collaboration with similarly coiffed DJ Pennyroyal (he of 1angreedwarf fame) is less peanut butter and chocolate and more chocolate with even more chocolate and washed down with a soda water from a Fizzie's secret stash (I suggest Dr. Pfeffer). Your teeth will rot and your pancreas will scream and you'll actually see time itself - and that's a good thing! Good for clearing out a room of assholes and seeing which ones remain. THOSE people are your real friends. Witchita Slayers slays, and not just in Foundation!"
-- Hellworld Untd. Issue 2 Vol. 2

Defeat the Day

"... Defeat the Day is supposedly the third part of a rumored quadrilogy of records (preceded by last decade's Languish the Earth and Kiss to Kill), but the mood and genre shifts so drastically between records it's hard to give the theory credence. We were in gothic blues territory by way of Swedish death-songs in the last album, but here the spirit of Phil Collins-era Genesis thrives. It's as though bastard pop has pushed through to the other side and became something else. Call it what you want; I call this outstanding."
-- Audiotheque Issue 6 Vol. 28
